1984 - Play Scenario

Scenario Overview

Setting: Oceania, 1984. A totalitarian society controlled by the Party, where Big Brother watches everything and independent thought is a crime.

Objective: Navigate the surveillance state, maintain your cover as a loyal Party member, and either resist the system or survive within it.

Victory Conditions: Control key territories, influence major characters, and either expose the Party's lies or maintain your position in the system.

Characters

Winston Smith

Role: Protagonist and Rebel
A Party member who secretly hates the Party and begins to question its control. Works at the Ministry of Truth rewriting historical records. Represents the individual's struggle against totalitarianism.

Julia

Role: Winston's Lover
A young woman who works in the Fiction Department and becomes Winston's lover. She represents personal rebellion and the human desire for love and freedom.

O'Brien

Role: Party Member and Interrogator
A high-ranking Party member who poses as a resistance leader to trap Winston. Represents the Party's power to manipulate and destroy individual thought.

Big Brother

Role: Supreme Leader
The omnipresent leader of Oceania who may or may not exist. Represents the absolute power of the Party and the cult of personality in totalitarian regimes.

Syme

Role: Winston's Colleague
A Party member who works on the Newspeak dictionary. Represents the Party's control over language and thought. His disappearance foreshadows Winston's fate.

Parsons

Role: Winston's Neighbor
A Party member who represents the indoctrinated masses. His arrest by his own children shows the Party's success in turning family members against each other.

Emmanuel Goldstein

Role: Enemy of the State
The supposed leader of the Brotherhood resistance movement. Represents the Party's need for an external enemy to maintain control and unity.

Mr. Charrington

Role: Shopkeeper and Thought Police
A seemingly harmless shopkeeper who is actually a Thought Police agent. Represents the Party's ability to infiltrate every aspect of life.

The Proles

Role: Working Class
The working class who make up 85% of the population. They are largely ignored by the Party but represent the potential for revolution.

Territories

Ministry of Truth

Controlled by: The Party
Where Winston works rewriting historical records. Represents the Party's control over truth and the manipulation of information.

Winston's Apartment

Controlled by: Winston (with surveillance)
Winston's private space where he writes in his diary and thinks forbidden thoughts. Represents the individual's inner world under surveillance.

The Telescreen

Controlled by: The Party
The omnipresent surveillance device that watches and listens to everyone. Represents the Party's total control and the loss of privacy.

Mr. Charrington's Shop

Controlled by: Thought Police
A seemingly innocent shop that becomes a trap for Winston and Julia. Represents the Party's ability to turn any space into a surveillance zone.

The Prole District

Controlled by: The Proles
The working-class area where Winston and Julia meet. Represents the potential for resistance and the Party's neglect of the masses.

Room 101

Controlled by: The Party
The ultimate torture chamber where Winston faces his greatest fear. Represents the Party's power to destroy the individual completely.

The Two Minutes Hate

Controlled by: The Party
The daily ritual where Party members express hatred for enemies. Represents the Party's use of emotional manipulation to maintain control.

The Chestnut Tree Café

Controlled by: The Party
Where Winston sits after his transformation, drinking Victory Gin. Represents the final stage of Party control and the destruction of the individual.
