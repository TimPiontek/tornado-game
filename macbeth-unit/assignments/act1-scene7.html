Macbeth Act 1 Scene 7 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Macbeth's castle at Inverness. A room in the castle where Macbeth is alone.
What Happens: Macbeth delivers his famous soliloquy, wrestling with his conscience about killing Duncan. He lists all the reasons why he shouldn't kill the king: Duncan is his kinsman, his guest, and a good king. He fears the consequences and the example it would set. Lady Macbeth enters and challenges his manhood when he tells her he has decided not to proceed. She convinces him to go through with the murder by questioning his courage and love for her. She outlines her plan to drug the guards and frame them for the murder.
Characters: Macbeth, Lady Macbeth, A servant (who announces Duncan's approach).
