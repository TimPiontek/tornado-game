Hamlet Act 1, Scene 4: The Ghost Returns

Scene Summary

Hamlet joins Horatio and Marcellus on the battlements. They hear the sounds of the king's revelry below, and Hamlet comments on the Danish custom of heavy drinking. The ghost appears again, and Hamlet follows it despite the warnings of his companions. The ghost beckons Hamlet to follow it to a more private location, and Hamlet, determined to speak with his father's spirit, follows the ghost into the darkness.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Hamlet - The prince, determined to speak with the ghost
  • Horatio - Hamlet's friend, warning him about the ghost
  • Marcellus - A guard who has witnessed the ghost
  • The Ghost - The spirit of King Hamlet

Multiple Choice Questions

1. Hamlet criticizes the Danish custom of heavy drinking, saying it "makes us traduced and taxed of other nations." What theme is shown here?
Answer: B. The theme of national identity and reputation
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme analysis.
2. The ghost beckons Hamlet to follow. What is the effect on tone?
Answer: B. It shifts to a tense and ominous tone.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Analyze tone.
3. Marcellus says, "Something is rotten in the state of Denmark." Which literary device is used?
Answer: A. Metaphor
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Analyze figurative language.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: Why do Horatio and Marcellus try to stop Hamlet from following the ghost?
Answer: They fear the ghost may tempt Hamlet toward madness or death: "It might deprive your sovereignty of reason / And draw you into madness."
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze interactions between characters.
5. Short Answer: What does Hamlet's decision to follow the ghost reveal about his character?
Answer: Hamlet is driven by loyalty and a desire for truth, even at personal risk. His resolve to follow demonstrates courage and desperation.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character development.
