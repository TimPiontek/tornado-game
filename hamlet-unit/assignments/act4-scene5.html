Hamlet Act 4, Scene 5: Ophelia's Madness

Scene Summary

Ophelia appears in a state of madness, singing fragments of songs about death and betrayal. Her madness is a stark contrast to Hamlet's feigned madness and reveals the devastating effects of her father's death and Hamlet's rejection. Laertes returns from France, furious about his father's death and his sister's condition. He vows revenge against Hamlet, creating another parallel revenge plot.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Ophelia - Polonius's daughter, now mad with grief
  • Laertes - Ophelia's brother, seeking revenge
  • Claudius - The king, dealing with the crisis
  • Gertrude - The queen, witnessing Ophelia's madness

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Ophelia's speech reveal truths that others cannot speak openly?
Answer: Through fragmented songs about betrayal and death, Ophelia exposes the corruption of the court. Her madness allows her to speak what protocol would normally forbid.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Analyze language for deeper meaning.
5. Short Answer: What does Laertes's reaction to Ophelia's condition reveal about his values?
Answer: Laertes's grief and outrage show that family loyalty motivates him more than politics, making him Hamlet's mirror in the pursuit of revenge.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character development.
