Hamlet Act 5 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Elsinore Castle, Denmark. Act 5 is the resolution of the play, containing the climactic duel and multiple deaths.
What Happens: Hamlet returns to Denmark and encounters gravediggers preparing Ophelia's grave. He reflects on death and mortality while holding Yorick's skull. Ophelia's funeral takes place, and Hamlet confronts Laertes. Claudius plots with Laertes to kill Hamlet in a fencing match. The duel takes place, and both Hamlet and Laertes are wounded by the poisoned sword. Gertrude drinks the poisoned wine and dies. Hamlet kills Claudius and then dies. Fortinbras arrives and takes control of Denmark. The play ends with Hamlet's body being carried off with military honors.
Characters: Hamlet (dying hero), Claudius (killed by Hamlet), Gertrude (poisoned), Laertes (killed in duel), Ophelia (buried), Horatio (surviving friend), Fortinbras (new king), The Gravediggers (comic relief), Osric (courtier), Yorick (skull).
