Hamlet Act 4, Scene 7: The Plot

Scene Summary

Claudius and Laertes plot Hamlet's death. Claudius manipulates Laertes into seeking revenge for Polonius's death. They plan a fencing match where Laertes will use a poisoned sword, and Claudius will prepare poisoned wine as a backup. News arrives that Ophelia has drowned, further fueling Laertes's rage and determination for revenge.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Claudius - The king, plotting Hamlet's death
  • Laertes - Ophelia's brother, seeking revenge
  • Gertrude - The queen, reporting Ophelia's death

Multiple Choice Questions

1. Why does Claudius manipulate Laertes into avenging Polonius's death?
Answer: B. To eliminate Hamlet without being blamed
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character motivation.
2. What does Laertes's willingness to "cut [Hamlet's] throat i' the church" reveal about his character?
Answer: B. His desire for revenge overrides moral or spiritual law
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme of revenge and morality.
3. What is the effect of Ophelia's death on Laertes?
Answer: B. He becomes consumed by grief and rage
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Emotional development.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Claudius's plan to poison the sword and cup reinforce the theme of corruption?
Answer: Claudius uses deception and hidden poison to secure power, symbolizing how corruption is concealed beneath appearances in Denmark.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme analysis.
5. Short Answer: What symbolic significance is there in Ophelia's drowning?
Answer: Her death by water reflects her helplessness and the overwhelming weight of grief. Her passive death contrasts with the active, bloody revenge of the male characters.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Symbolism analysis.
