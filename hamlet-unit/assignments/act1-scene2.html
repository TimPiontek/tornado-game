Hamlet Act 1, Scene 2: The Court Scene

Scene Summary

This scene takes place in the royal court of Denmark. King Claudius addresses the court, acknowledging both the death of his brother King Hamlet and his recent marriage to Queen Gertrude. He also deals with the threat from young Fortinbras of Norway. Hamlet is introduced for the first time, appearing in mourning clothes and expressing his deep grief over his father's death and his mother's hasty remarriage. Claudius and Gertrude try to convince Hamlet to stop mourning and accept the new situation. Hamlet's first soliloquy reveals his suicidal thoughts and his disgust with the world, particularly his mother's quick remarriage to his uncle.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • King Claudius - The new king, Hamlet's uncle and stepfather
  • Queen Gertrude - Hamlet's mother, recently married to Claudius
  • Hamlet - The prince, mourning his father's death
  • Polonius - The chief counselor to the king
  • Laertes - Polonius's son, asking permission to return to France
  • Voltemand & Cornelius - Courtiers sent to Norway

Multiple Choice Questions

1. In Claudius's speech, he says: "Though yet of Hamlet our dear brother's death The memory be green…" What is Claudius attempting to achieve with this opening?
Answer: B. He is publicly acknowledging grief to justify his swift marriage to Gertrude.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze how a character's choices develop the plot.
2. Claudius speaks of young Fortinbras as "puffed with divine ambition." What does this line reveal about Claudius's view of Fortinbras?
Answer: C. Claudius believes Fortinbras is overly confident and a threat.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Interpret figurative language and connotations.
3. Hamlet responds to Gertrude: "Seems, madam? Nay, it is. I know not 'seems.'" What is Hamlet criticizing?
Answer: B. The idea that grief can be performed as a display
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Analyze theme development.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Hamlet's first soliloquy ("O that this too too solid flesh would melt…") reveal his internal conflict? Use a direct quote.
Answer: Hamlet wishes his "too too solid flesh would melt," revealing suicidal despair over his mother's marriage and the corruption he sees in the world. He feels powerless and disgusted, calling the world "an unweeded garden."
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze complex character development.
5. Short Answer: What contrast does Shakespeare establish between Claudius and Hamlet's perspectives on grief?
Answer: Claudius dismisses prolonged grief as "unmanly," while Hamlet views grief as a sincere emotional response. This contrast highlights Claudius's political pragmatism versus Hamlet's moral depth.
Standard: RL.9-10.6 – Analyze point of view.
