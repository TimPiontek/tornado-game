Hamlet Act 5, Scene 1: The Gravediggers

Scene Summary

Two gravediggers discuss death and the law while preparing Ophelia's grave. Hamlet and Horatio arrive, and Hamlet reflects on mortality while holding the skull of Yorick, the court jester from his childhood. This scene provides comic relief while also exploring themes of death and the equality of all people in death.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Gravediggers - Two men preparing Ophelia's grave
  • Hamlet - The prince, reflecting on mortality
  • Horatio - Hamlet's friend, witnessing the scene
  • Laertes - Ophelia's brother, arriving for the funeral
  • Claudius - The king, attending the funeral
  • Gertrude - The queen, mourning Ophelia

Multiple Choice Questions

1. As Hamlet holds Yorick's skull, he says, "Alas, poor Yorick! I knew him, Horatio." What theme is Shakespeare emphasizing?
Answer: B. The inevitability and equality of death
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Determine theme and analyze its development.
2. The Gravedigger speaks in riddles and jokes while digging graves. What tone does this create?
Answer: B. Cynical acceptance of death
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Analyze how tone is created through dialogue.
3. When Laertes leaps into Ophelia's grave, what emotion primarily drives his dramatic action?
Answer: C. Grief mixed with rage and desperation
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze character motivations and their effect on the plot.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does the imagery of the skulls in this scene contribute to Hamlet's existential crisis?
Answer: The skulls remind Hamlet that death strips away identity and power. When he imagines Alexander the Great reduced to dust stopping a beer barrel, he confronts the futility of worldly ambition.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Figurative language and meaning.
5. Short Answer: What does this scene reveal about Hamlet's transformation in his attitude toward death?
Answer: Hamlet shifts from fearing death to accepting it as natural and universal. This acceptance empowers him to face his fate in the final act.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character development.
