Hamlet Act 3, Scene 2: The Mousetrap

Scene Summary

Hamlet instructs the players on how to perform the play and adds a speech that mirrors his father's murder. During the performance, Claudius becomes visibly upset and leaves abruptly, confirming Hamlet's suspicions about his guilt. Hamlet is now certain that Claudius murdered his father. Rosencrantz and Guildenstern try to get information from Hamlet, but he sees through their deception.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Hamlet - The prince, orchestrating the play to test Claudius
  • The Players - Actors performing the play-within-a-play
  • Claudius - The king, reacting to the play
  • Gertrude - The queen, watching the performance
  • Polonius - The counselor, also watching
  • Ophelia - Polonius's daughter, Hamlet's former love
  • Rosencrantz & Guildenstern - Hamlet's friends, now spies

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does the play-within-a-play serve as a turning point in the plot?
Answer: It confirms Claudius's guilt, transitioning the play from suspicion to certainty. After Claudius flees, Hamlet is no longer questioning whether the ghost was truthful.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme and plot development.
5. Short Answer: Why does Hamlet speak in prose to Polonius but in verse when speaking privately or to Horatio? What does this shift indicate?
Answer: Hamlet uses prose to mock or belittle those he sees as inferior, such as Polonius, but uses verse when expressing his true thoughts. This shift reflects the theme of appearance vs. reality.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Analyze language choices.
