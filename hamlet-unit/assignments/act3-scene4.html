Hamlet Act 3, Scene 4: The Closet Scene

Scene Summary

Hamlet confronts his mother in her private chamber, accusing her of complicity in his father's murder. The ghost of King Hamlet appears, visible only to Hamlet, reminding him of his mission but urging him not to harm his mother. Gertrude cannot see the ghost and believes Hamlet is truly mad. Polonius, who was hiding behind a curtain, is killed by Hamlet when he thinks it is Claudius.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Hamlet - The prince, confronting his mother
  • Gertrude - The queen, Hamlet's mother
  • The Ghost - King Hamlet's spirit, appearing to Hamlet
  • Polonius - The counselor, killed by Hamlet

Multiple Choice Questions

1. When Hamlet says to Gertrude, "You go not till I set you up a glass / Where you may see the inmost part of you," what is he implying?
Answer: B. He will reveal her guilt and moral corruption.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Analyze figurative meaning and tone.
2. Why does Hamlet kill Polonius?
Answer: A. He mistakes Polonius for Claudius behind the arras.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze character motivation and its effect on the plot.
3. The Ghost appears to Hamlet during his confrontation with Gertrude. What is the purpose of this reappearance?
Answer: A. To remind Hamlet of his mission and refocus him on revenge.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Analyze theme development (revenge vs. conscience).

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Hamlet's treatment of Gertrude in this scene reveal both his anger and his moral struggle?
Answer: Hamlet attacks his mother's choices, calling her marriage "such an act / That blurs the grace and blush of modesty," but he does not physically harm her. His anger coexists with a desire for her repentance, revealing internal conflict.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze complex character emotions.
5. Short Answer: What does the Ghost's invisibility to Gertrude suggest about her and Hamlet's states of mind?
Answer: Gertrude's inability to see the Ghost shows her moral blindness, while Hamlet's vision suggests heightened awareness. It deepens the theme of perception versus reality.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme analysis.
