Hamlet Act 2, Scene 1: Polonius and Reynaldo

Scene Summary

Polonius sends his servant Reynaldo to spy on Laertes in France, instructing him to spread false rumors about Laertes to see how people react. This reveals Polonius's manipulative nature. Ophelia enters and reports that Hamlet has been acting strangely, appearing disheveled and speaking incoherently. Polonius concludes that Hamlet's madness is caused by his love for Ophelia.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Polonius - The chief counselor, sending Reynaldo to spy
  • Reynaldo - Polonius's servant, sent to spy on Laertes
  • Ophelia - Polonius's daughter, reporting Hamlet's strange behavior

Multiple Choice Questions

1. What is the purpose of Polonius sending Reynaldo to spy on Laertes?
Answer: C. To control his son's reputation through deceit
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze development of characters and motivations.
2. When Ophelia describes Hamlet's disheveled appearance, what does it primarily indicate about his state of mind?
Answer: B. He is performing madness to manipulate others
Standard: RL.9-10.1 – Cite textual evidence to support interpretations.
3. Ophelia says: "He took me by the wrist and held me hard." Why is this scene important to the theme of appearance vs. reality?
Answer: B. It suggests his madness may be an act directed at communicating distress
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme analysis.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Polonius's interpretation of Hamlet's behavior reveal his own flaws as a character?
Answer: Polonius assumes Hamlet's actions stem from "the very ecstasy of love," showing his arrogance and tendency to reduce complex behavior to simple causes, highlighting his lack of insight.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Characterization.
5. Short Answer: What dramatic purpose does Ophelia's report of Hamlet's behavior serve?
Answer: It heightens tension and introduces the motif of madness, prompting questions about whether Hamlet's madness is genuine or strategic.
Standard: RL.9-10.6 – Analyze point of view and its impact on meaning.
