Hamlet Act 4 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Elsinore Castle, Denmark. Act 4 deals with the consequences of Polonius's death and the escalating tension.
What Happens: Claudius learns of Polonius's death and sends Hamlet to England with Rosencrantz and Guildenstern. Ophelia goes mad with grief over her father's death. Laertes returns from France seeking revenge for his father's death. Claudius plots with Laertes to kill Hamlet. Ophelia drowns, possibly by suicide. Hamlet returns to Denmark and encounters Fortinbras's army. The act ends with Hamlet's famous soliloquy about Fortinbras and his own inaction.
Characters: Hamlet (sent to England, returns), Claudius (plotting with Laertes), Ophelia (mad with grief, drowns), Laertes (seeking revenge), Gertrude (reporting Ophelia's death), Rosencrantz and Guildenstern (escorting Hamlet), Fortinbras (Norwegian prince), The Gravediggers (preparing Ophelia's grave).
