Hamlet Act 4, Scene 6: Hamlet's Letter

Scene Summary

Horatio receives a letter from Hamlet describing his escape from the ship bound for England. Hamlet discovered Claudius's secret orders for his execution and managed to escape. He asks Horatio to come to him and promises to tell him more about what happened. This scene shows Hamlet's resourcefulness and his continued trust in Horatio.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Horatio - Hamlet's friend, receiving the letter
  • Hamlet - The prince, writing from his escape
    • Gentleman - Delivering Hamlet's letter

Multiple Choice Questions

1. What does Hamlet's escape from the ship reveal about his character?
Answer: B. He is resourceful and willing to act directly
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character development.
2. Hamlet says he discovered "a royal knavery" in the commission. What is he referring to?
Answer: A. A letter ordering his execution
Standard: RL.9-10.1 – Cite evidence.
3. Hamlet's letter ends by saying he has "words to speak in thine ear." What dramatic purpose does this serve?
Answer: A. It foreshadows new knowledge that will shift the plot
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Plot structure.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Hamlet's escape mark a shift in his approach to fate?
Answer: Hamlet now acts upon opportunity rather than waiting for perfect certainty. His actions suggest acceptance of fate as an active force.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme analysis (fate vs free will).
5. Short Answer: Why is Horatio the one Hamlet writes to first after his escape?
Answer: Horatio is Hamlet's trusted moral anchor and the only character he believes capable of understanding the truth and preserving it.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character relationship analysis.
