Hamlet Act 2, Scene 2: The Spies Arrive

Scene Summary

Claudius and Gertrude welcome Rosencrantz and Guildenstern, Hamlet's childhood friends, and ask them to spy on Hamlet to discover the cause of his apparent madness. Polonius announces that he has found the cause: Hamlet's love for Ophelia. Polonius suggests they spy on a meeting between Hamlet and Ophelia. A group of traveling players arrives, and Hamlet asks them to perform a play that he will modify to reenact his father's murder.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Claudius - The king, arranging spies to watch Hamlet
  • Gertrude - The queen, concerned about Hamlet
  • Rosencrantz & Guildenstern - Hamlet's childhood friends, now spies
  • Polonius - The chief counselor, claiming to know the cause
  • Hamlet - The prince, planning to use the players
  • The Players - Traveling actors

Multiple Choice Questions

1. Hamlet calls Denmark a "prison." What does this metaphor reveal?
Answer: B. He feels trapped by his circumstances and unable to act freely.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Interpret metaphors and connotations.
2. When Hamlet says to Polonius: "You are a fishmonger," how is dramatic irony used?
Answer: B. Hamlet insults Polonius while Polonius thinks he is being respected.
Standard: RL.9-10.6 – Analyze irony.
3. What does the "What a piece of work is a man" speech reveal about Hamlet?
Answer: C. He is disillusioned with humanity and sees no value in life.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme development.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: Why is the arrival of the players significant to Hamlet's plan?
Answer: The players give Hamlet a means to "catch the conscience of the king" through performance, using theater as a moral test to reveal Claudius's guilt.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze plot development.
5. Short Answer: Hamlet refers to himself as "a rogue and peasant slave." What does this reveal about his internal conflict?
Answer: Hamlet feels ashamed of his inaction. By calling himself a "slave," he suggests he is controlled by fear and indecision despite having a moral duty to act.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze character psychology.
