Hamlet Act 2, Scene 2: The Spies Arrive

Scene Summary

Claudius and Gertrude welcome Rosencrantz and Guildenstern, Hamlet's childhood friends, and ask them to spy on Hamlet to discover the cause of his apparent madness. Polonius announces that he has found the cause: Hamlet's love for Ophelia. Polonius suggests they spy on a meeting between Hamlet and Ophelia. A group of traveling players arrives, and Hamlet asks them to perform a play that he will modify to reenact his father's murder.
