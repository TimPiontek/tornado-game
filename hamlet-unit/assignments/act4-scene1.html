Hamlet Act 4, Scene 1: Polonius's Death

Scene Summary

Gertrude informs Claudius that Hamlet has killed Polonius. Claudius realizes that Hamlet's madness has turned violent and that he himself could be in danger. He decides to send Hamlet to England immediately, ostensibly for his safety but actually to have him killed. This scene marks a turning point as Claudius moves from trying to understand Hamlet's madness to actively plotting his death.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Gertrude - The queen, reporting Polonius's death
  • Claudius - The king, plotting Hamlet's death

Multiple Choice Questions

1. How does Gertrude describe Hamlet's mental state to Claudius?

2. Claudius's line, "It had been so with us, had we been there," reveals what about his fears?
Answer: A. He fears Hamlet's madness could have turned on him.

3. What theme is reinforced by Claudius's decision to send Hamlet to England?
Answer: B. Political self-preservation and corruption

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Gertrude's behavior here differ from earlier in the play, and what does it suggest about her development?
Answer: Gertrude conceals the truth about the Ghost and shows fear rather than denial, suggesting she now sees Claudius's corruption and begins to sympathize with Hamlet.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character development.
5. Short Answer: How does Claudius's reaction to Polonius's death advance the play's tension?
Answer: Instead of mourning Polonius, Claudius worries about himself and the kingdom's perception. His self-interest intensifies the sense of corruption and danger surrounding the throne.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme development.
