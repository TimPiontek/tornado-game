Hamlet Act 1, Scene 1: The Ghost Appears

Scene Summary

The play opens on the battlements of Elsinore Castle in Denmark. It is midnight, and Francisco, a guard, is being relieved by Bernardo. The two guards discuss the strange events of the past few nights, during which they have seen what appears to be the ghost of the recently deceased King Hamlet. Marcellus and Horatio arrive, and Horatio, a scholar and Hamlet's close friend, is skeptical about the ghost's existence. However, when the ghost appears, Horatio recognizes it as the late king and attempts to speak to it. The ghost does not respond and disappears at dawn. This supernatural encounter sets the tone for the play and introduces the central mystery of King Hamlet's death.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Francisco - A guard on duty
  • Bernardo - A guard who has seen the ghost
  • Marcellus - Another guard who has witnessed the ghost
  • Horatio - Hamlet's friend and a scholar, skeptical of the supernatural
  • The Ghost - The spirit of King Hamlet, Hamlet's father

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does the ghost's silence contribute to the theme of uncertainty in this scene? Use evidence from the text.
Answer: The ghost's refusal to speak creates an atmosphere of fear and ambiguity. Horatio notes that if the ghost "assume my noble father's person," it should be able to speak, yet its silence implies hidden truths are being suppressed, contributing to the theme of political corruption and unknown danger.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Analyze theme development through textual evidence.
5. Short Answer: What does the watchmen's insistence on bringing Horatio to witness the ghost reveal about their beliefs and fears?
Answer: The watchmen believe that Horatio, as a scholar, has the authority and rational mind to interpret the ghost's appearance. This demonstrates their fear that the ghost is a warning and shows the tension between reason and superstition: "Thou art a scholar; speak to it, Horatio."
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze character motivations and their impact on the plot.
