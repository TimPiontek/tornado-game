Hamlet Act 5, Scene 2: The Final Duel

Scene Summary

The climactic scene where Hamlet, Laertes, Claudius, and Gertrude all die. Hamlet reveals Claudius's treachery to Horatio and the court. During the fencing match, Laertes wounds Hamlet with the poisoned sword, but Hamlet also wounds Laertes. Gertrude drinks the poisoned wine meant for Hamlet. In his dying moments, Hamlet kills Claudius and asks Horatio to tell his story. Fortinbras arrives and takes control of Denmark.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Hamlet - The prince, finally achieving revenge
  • Laertes - Ophelia's brother, seeking revenge
  • Claudius - The king, finally killed by Hamlet
  • Gertrude - The queen, dies from poisoned wine
  • Horatio - Hamlet's friend, survives to tell the story
  • Fortinbras - Prince of Norway, takes control of Denmark
  • Osric - A courtier, arranges the duel

Multiple Choice Questions

1. Hamlet says to Horatio, "There's a divinity that shapes our ends." What change in Hamlet's worldview is reflected here?
Answer: B. He submits to divine providence over human planning.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Analyze theme of fate vs free will.
2. Why does Laertes say, "I am justly killed with mine own treachery"?
Answer: B. He admits his betrayal has led to his death, acknowledging moral justice.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character transformation and recognition.
3. What is the dramatic significance of Fortinbras arriving at the end?
Answer: B. He provides political stability and closure after the royal family's death.
Standard: RL.9-10.5 – Analyze how structure contributes to theme and resolution.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: How does Hamlet's killing of Claudius resolve the central conflict?
Answer: Hamlet finally fulfills the Ghost's command, ending the chain of corruption. However, his delayed action results in tragedy for nearly everyone, emphasizing the cost of moral hesitation.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Analyze resolution of character arc.
5. Short Answer: Why is it significant that Hamlet gives his "dying voice" to Fortinbras?
Answer: Hamlet acknowledges Fortinbras as a rightful and honorable successor, indicating Hamlet's final acceptance of political order and fate. It creates thematic closure and restores stability.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme and conclusion analysis.
