Hamlet Act 4, Scene 2: Rosencrantz and Guildenstern

Scene Summary

Rosencrantz and Guildenstern confront Hamlet about Polonius's body. Hamlet mocks them with wordplay and refuses to tell them where the body is hidden. He calls Rosencrantz a "sponge" and reveals that he sees through their deception. This scene shows Hamlet's growing awareness of the corruption around him and his increasing isolation.

Key Characters in This Scene

  • Hamlet - The prince, mocking his former friends
  • Rosencrantz - Hamlet's childhood friend, now a spy
  • Guildenstern - Hamlet's childhood friend, now a spy

Multiple Choice Questions

1. Hamlet says to Rosencrantz, "The body is with the king, but the king is not with the body." What literary device is he using?
Answer: A. Irony
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Interpret figurative and ironic meaning.
2. How does Hamlet's tone toward Rosencrantz and Guildenstern differ from his tone toward Horatio?
Answer: B. He mocks and manipulates them as tools of Claudius.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character relationships and tone.
3. Hamlet calls Rosencrantz a "sponge." What does he mean by this metaphor?
Answer: B. Rosencrantz absorbs the king's favors and will be wrung dry when no longer useful.
Standard: RL.9-10.4 – Analyze figurative language and meaning.

Short Answer Questions

4. Short Answer: What does Hamlet's wordplay in this scene reveal about his state of mind?
Answer: His dark humor and riddling speech show both wit and psychological strain. He uses irony to maintain control, mocking those who underestimate him.
Standard: RL.9-10.3 – Character psychology.
5. Short Answer: How does this scene develop the theme of manipulation and deceit?
Answer: Hamlet's refusal to reveal Polonius's body mirrors Claudius's secrecy, showing how deception has infected everyone in the court. Both sides now use lies as survival tools.
Standard: RL.9-10.2 – Theme development.
