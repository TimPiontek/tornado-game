Animal Farm Chapter 9 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Animal Farm. The animals face increasing hardship and Boxer's tragic fate reveals the true nature of Napoleon's regime.
What Happens: The animals work harder than ever but receive less food. Boxer works himself to exhaustion and collapses. Napoleon promises to send him to a veterinarian, but instead sells him to a glue factory. Squealer lies to the animals, claiming Boxer died peacefully in a hospital. The pigs begin to walk on two legs and wear clothes, becoming indistinguishable from humans. The chapter ends with the animals seeing Napoleon and the pigs walking on two legs, completely betraying the original principles of Animalism.
Characters: Boxer (dying from overwork), Napoleon (selling Boxer to glue factory), Squealer (lying about Boxer's death), Clover (horrified but confused), Benjamin (skeptical), The sheep (repeating new slogans), The other animals (terrified and confused), The pigs (walking on two legs, wearing clothes).
