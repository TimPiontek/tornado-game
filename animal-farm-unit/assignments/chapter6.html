Animal Farm Chapter 6 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Animal Farm. The animals work harder than ever to build the windmill, facing increasing hardship and manipulation.
What Happens: The animals work harder than ever to build the windmill, working on Sundays without complaint. Napoleon announces that the farm will trade with neighboring farms, violating the original principles. The pigs move into the farmhouse and sleep in beds, claiming it's necessary for their work. Squealer convinces the animals that these changes are actually in line with the original principles. The windmill is destroyed in a storm, and Napoleon blames Snowball. The animals are forced to work even harder to rebuild it.
Characters: Napoleon (increasingly authoritarian), Squealer (manipulating the truth), Boxer (working harder than ever), Clover (concerned but confused), Benjamin (skeptical), The sheep (repeating slogans), The other animals (working harder), Mr. Whymper (human trader), Snowball (blamed for everything).
