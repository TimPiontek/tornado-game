Animal Farm Chapter 3 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Animal Farm, during the first summer after the rebellion. The animals are working hard to establish their new society.
What Happens: The animals work harder than ever before, with the harvest being more successful than under Mr. Jones. The pigs begin to take on leadership roles, with Snowball organizing committees and Napoleon taking charge of the puppies' education. The pigs start to take the milk and apples for themselves, claiming they need them for "brain work." Squealer justifies this by saying the pigs are working for the good of all animals. The animals accept this explanation, showing the beginning of inequality.
Characters: Snowball (organizing committees), Napoleon (educating puppies), Squealer (propagandist), Boxer (working harder than ever), Clover (concerned about the pigs' privileges), Mollie (avoiding work), Benjamin (skeptical), Muriel (reading the commandments), The sheep (easily manipulated), The puppies (being trained by Napoleon).
