Animal Farm Chapter 10 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Animal Farm. The final chapter reveals the complete transformation of the farm and the ultimate betrayal of the revolution.
What Happens: Years have passed, and the farm has been completely transformed. The pigs have become indistinguishable from humans, walking on two legs, wearing clothes, and carrying whips. The windmill is finally completed, but it's used to mill corn for profit rather than generating electricity for the animals. The original commandments have been replaced with a single commandment: "All animals are equal, but some animals are more equal than others." The pigs host a dinner party with humans, and the animals can no longer tell the difference between pigs and humans. The revolution has completely failed.
Characters: Napoleon (now indistinguishable from humans), Squealer (justifying all changes), Clover (horrified but confused), Benjamin (skeptical), The sheep (repeating new slogans), The other animals (terrified and confused), The pigs (walking on two legs, wearing clothes, carrying whips), The humans (dining with the pigs).
