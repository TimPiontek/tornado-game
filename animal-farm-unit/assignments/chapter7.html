Animal Farm Chapter 7 - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Animal Farm. The animals face severe hardship and Napoleon's increasing tyranny reaches new heights.
What Happens: The animals face severe food shortages and harsh winter conditions. Napoleon announces that the farm will sell eggs to humans, violating the original principles. The hens rebel against this decision, but Napoleon starves them into submission. Napoleon holds a show trial where animals confess to crimes they didn't commit and are executed. The animals are terrified and confused. The chapter ends with the animals singing "Beasts of England" being banned and replaced with a new song praising Napoleon.
Characters: Napoleon (increasingly tyrannical), Squealer (justifying atrocities), Boxer (working harder than ever), Clover (horrified but confused), Benjamin (skeptical), The hens (rebelling against egg sales), The sheep (repeating new slogans), The other animals (terrified and confused), The executed animals (confessing to false crimes).
