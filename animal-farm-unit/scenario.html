Animal Farm - Play Scenario

Scenario Overview

Setting: Animal Farm, a once-prosperous farm now under animal control after the successful rebellion against Mr. Jones.

Objective: Control the farm and maintain the principles of Animalism while dealing with internal power struggles and external threats.

Victory Conditions: Establish dominance over the farm, control key territories, and maintain the loyalty of the animal population.

Characters

Napoleon

Role: Dictator Pig
The main antagonist who seizes power through manipulation and force. Controls the farm through fear, propaganda, and his private army of dogs. Represents Joseph Stalin.

Snowball

Role: Revolutionary Pig
The idealistic pig who initially shares power with Napoleon. Advocates for education and technological advancement. Represents Leon Trotsky.

Squealer

Role: Propaganda Minister
Napoleon's right-hand pig who uses persuasive language to justify the pigs' actions and maintain control over the other animals.

Boxer

Role: Working Class Hero
The loyal cart-horse who represents the working class. His motto "I will work harder" shows his dedication but also his exploitation.

Clover

Role: Concerned Mare
A mare who questions the pigs' actions but lacks the courage to speak out. Represents the silent majority who are aware of injustice but don't act.

Benjamin

Role: Cynical Donkey
The oldest and most cynical animal who sees through the pigs' lies but chooses not to act. Represents intellectuals who remain passive.

Mollie

Role: Bourgeois Mare
A white mare who represents the bourgeoisie. She cares more about her appearance and comfort than the revolution's ideals.

Moses

Role: Religious Raven
A raven who spreads stories about "Sugarcandy Mountain," representing organized religion as a tool of control.

Territories

The Farmhouse

Controlled by: Pigs
The former human residence, now preserved as a museum but secretly used by the pigs. Represents the seat of power and privilege.

The Barn

Controlled by: All Animals
The central meeting place where the Seven Commandments are displayed. The site of major decisions and propaganda sessions.

The Windmill

Controlled by: Pigs (claimed by all)
The symbol of progress and hope, built through the animals' hard work. Represents industrialization and the promise of a better future.

The Pasture

Controlled by: Working Animals
The main grazing area where the animals work and live. Represents the means of production and the source of the farm's wealth.

The Orchard

Controlled by: Pigs
The source of apples, claimed by the pigs for their "brain work." Represents the unequal distribution of resources.

The Dairy

Controlled by: Pigs
Where the milk is produced and claimed by the pigs. Represents the exploitation of the working class's labor.

The Kennels

Controlled by: Napoleon's Dogs
The training ground for Napoleon's private army. Represents the use of force and intimidation to maintain power.

The Forest Edge

Controlled by: Neutral Territory
The boundary between Animal Farm and the outside world. Represents the isolation and paranoia of totalitarian regimes.
