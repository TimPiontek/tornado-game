World War II - The Holocaust

Historical Context

Setting: Europe, 1933-1945. The systematic persecution and murder of six million Jews and millions of others by Nazi Germany and its collaborators.
What Happens: The Holocaust begins with the Nazi rise to power in 1933 and escalates throughout World War II. Jews and other targeted groups are subjected to discrimination, forced labor, ghettoization, and mass murder. The "Final Solution" is implemented, leading to the establishment of death camps like Auschwitz-Birkenau, Treblinka, and Sobibor. Millions of people are murdered in gas chambers, through starvation, disease, and other forms of systematic killing. The Holocaust ends with the liberation of the camps by Allied forces in 1945.
