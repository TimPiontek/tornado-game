American Revolution - Revolutionary War Impact

Historical Context

Setting: United States and Europe, 1775-1783. The social, economic, and political impact of the Revolutionary War on American society.
What Happens: The Revolutionary War has profound and lasting impacts on American society. It creates a new nation based on democratic principles and individual rights. The war disrupts traditional social hierarchies and creates opportunities for social mobility. It leads to economic changes including inflation, debt, and the need for new trade relationships. The war also affects women, African Americans, and Native Americans in different ways. The experience of war shapes American identity and values, establishing principles of liberty, equality, and self-government that continue to influence the nation.
Key Figures: George Washington (commander and future president), Thomas Jefferson (author of Declaration), John Adams (diplomat and future president), Benjamin Franklin (diplomat and inventor), Alexander Hamilton (aide-de-camp and future statesman), Marquis de Lafayette (French ally), Baron von Steuben (Prussian military advisor), Deborah Sampson (disguised soldier), Phillis Wheatley (poet), Crispus Attucks (first casualty), Joseph Brant (Native American leader), Molly Pitcher (camp follower).
