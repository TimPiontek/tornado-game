Ancient Greece - Military and Warfare

Historical Context

Setting: Ancient Greece, 800-300 BCE. The development of Greek military tactics and warfare that influenced military strategy for centuries.
What Happens: Ancient Greece develops innovative military tactics and warfare strategies that influence military thinking for centuries. The hoplite phalanx becomes the dominant infantry formation, emphasizing discipline and coordination. Greek city-states develop professional armies and naval forces. The Persian Wars demonstrate Greek military effectiveness against larger forces. The Peloponnesian War shows the evolution of Greek warfare tactics. Alexander the Great's conquests demonstrate the effectiveness of combined arms tactics. These military innovations establish principles of warfare that continue to influence modern military strategy.
Key Figures: Leonidas (Spartan king), Themistocles (Athenian general), Pericles (Athenian statesman), Alexander the Great (Macedonian king), Philip II (Macedonian king), Epaminondas (Theban general), Xenophon (general and historian), Alcibiades (Athenian general), Brasidas (Spartan general), Cleon (Athenian general), Nicias (Athenian general), Demosthenes (Athenian general), Iphicrates (Athenian general), Chabrias (Athenian general).
