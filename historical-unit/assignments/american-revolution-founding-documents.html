American Revolution - Founding Documents

Historical Context

Setting: United States, 1776-1791. The creation of foundational documents that establish the principles and structure of the new American government.
What Happens: The American Revolution produces foundational documents that establish the principles and structure of the new American government. The Declaration of Independence (1776) declares the colonies' independence and establishes principles of liberty and equality. The Articles of Confederation (1781) create the first national government. The Constitution (1787) establishes the framework for the federal government with separation of powers and checks and balances. The Bill of Rights (1791) protects individual liberties. These documents establish the foundation for American democracy and continue to influence American government and society.
Key Figures: Thomas Jefferson (author of Declaration), John Adams (advocate for independence), Benjamin Franklin (diplomat and philosopher), George Washington (presiding officer), James Madison (Father of the Constitution), Alexander Hamilton (Federalist leader), John Jay (diplomat and jurist), Roger Sherman (compromise architect), Gouverneur Morris (constitution writer), George Mason (Bill of Rights advocate), Patrick Henry (Anti-Federalist), Samuel Adams (revolutionary leader).
