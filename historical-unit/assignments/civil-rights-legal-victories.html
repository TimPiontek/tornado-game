Civil Rights Movement - Legal Victories

Historical Context

Setting: United States, 1950s-1960s. The legal battles and court victories that helped dismantle segregation and discrimination.
What Happens: The Civil Rights Movement achieves major legal victories through the court system. Brown v. Board of Education (1954) declares school segregation unconstitutional. The Civil Rights Act of 1964 bans discrimination in public accommodations and employment. The Voting Rights Act of 1965 removes barriers to voting. The Fair Housing Act of 1968 prohibits housing discrimination. These legal victories provide the foundation for social change and help dismantle the legal framework of segregation and discrimination.
Key Figures: Thurgood Marshall (NAACP lawyer), Earl Warren (Chief Justice), Lyndon Johnson (President), Martin Luther King Jr. (civil rights leader), Rosa Parks (civil rights activist), Oliver Brown (plaintiff in Brown v. Board), Linda Brown (student), John Lewis (civil rights leader), Fannie Lou Hamer (voting rights activist), Constance Baker Motley (civil rights lawyer).
