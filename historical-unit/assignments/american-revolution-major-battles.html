American Revolution - Major Battles

Historical Context

Setting: Various locations across the American colonies, 1776-1781. The war expands beyond New England to include major battles across the colonies.
What Happens: The war spreads across the colonies with major battles including the Battle of Long Island (1776), the Battle of Trenton (1776), the Battle of Saratoga (1777), the Battle of Monmouth (1778), and the Siege of Yorktown (1781). The Continental Army faces numerous challenges including harsh winters, lack of supplies, and desertions. The Battle of Saratoga proves to be a turning point, convincing France to enter the war on the American side. The war culminates in the Siege of Yorktown, where British forces under Cornwallis surrender.
Key Figures: George Washington (Continental Army commander), Horatio Gates (victor at Saratoga), Benedict Arnold (hero turned traitor), Charles Cornwallis (British general), William Howe (British general), Henry Clinton (British general), Nathanael Greene (Continental general), Daniel Morgan (Continental general), Friedrich von Steuben (Prussian drillmaster), Marquis de Lafayette (French ally).
