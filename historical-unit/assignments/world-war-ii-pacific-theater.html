World War II - Pacific Theater

Historical Context

Setting: Pacific Ocean and East Asia, 1941-1945. The war between Japan and the Allied powers in the Pacific region.
What Happens: The Pacific Theater begins with Japan's attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941. Japan rapidly expands across the Pacific, capturing territories including the Philippines, Singapore, and much of Southeast Asia. The Battle of Midway in 1942 marks a turning point as the US Navy defeats the Japanese fleet. The Allies begin island-hopping campaigns to retake territory. Major battles include Guadalcanal, Iwo Jima, and Okinawa. The war ends with the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki and Japan's surrender on September 2, 1945.
Key Figures: Douglas MacArthur (Allied commander), Chester Nimitz (US Navy commander), Isoroku Yamamoto (Japanese admiral), Hideki Tojo (Japanese Prime Minister), Emperor Hirohito (Japanese emperor), Harry Truman (US President), Winston Churchill (British Prime Minister), Joseph Stilwell (US general), William Halsey (US admiral), Raymond Spruance (US admiral).
