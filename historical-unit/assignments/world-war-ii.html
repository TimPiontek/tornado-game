World War II - Classroom Assignment

Story Summary

Setting: Global conflict from 1939-1945, involving most of the world's nations. The war was fought on multiple continents and oceans.
What Happens: World War II begins with Germany's invasion of Poland in 1939. The war expands across Europe, Asia, and the Pacific. Key events include the Holocaust, Pearl Harbor attack, D-Day invasion, Battle of Stalingrad, and the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. The war ends with the defeat of the Axis powers (Germany, Italy, Japan) by the Allied powers (United States, Britain, Soviet Union, France). The war results in over 70 million deaths and fundamentally changes the global political landscape.
Key Figures: Adolf Hitler (German dictator), Winston Churchill (British Prime Minister), Franklin D. Roosevelt (US President), Joseph Stalin (Soviet leader), Benito Mussolini (Italian dictator), Emperor Hirohito (Japanese emperor), Dwight D. Eisenhower (Allied commander), Douglas MacArthur (US general), Erwin Rommel (German general), Charles de Gaulle (French leader).
