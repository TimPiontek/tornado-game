American Revolution - Treaty of Paris

Historical Context

Setting: Paris, France, 1783. The negotiations to end the American Revolutionary War and establish peace between Britain and the United States.
What Happens: After the American victory at Yorktown, negotiations begin to end the war. The Treaty of Paris is signed on September 3, 1783, officially ending the American Revolutionary War. The treaty recognizes American independence and establishes the boundaries of the new nation. Britain agrees to withdraw all troops from American territory. The treaty also addresses issues of debt, property rights, and fishing rights. The signing marks the formal end of British rule in America and the beginning of the United States as an independent nation.
Key Figures: John Adams (American negotiator), Benjamin Franklin (American negotiator), John Jay (American negotiator), Henry Laurens (American negotiator), David Hartley (British negotiator), Richard Oswald (British negotiator), King George III (British king), George Washington (American commander), Lord Shelburne (British Prime Minister), Charles James Fox (British politician).
