Civil Rights Movement - Major Events

Historical Context

Setting: United States, 1960s. A period of intense activism and significant progress in the fight for civil rights.
What Happens: Major events include the Freedom Rides (1961), the Birmingham Campaign (1963), the March on Washington (1963), the Selma to Montgomery March (1965), and the passage of the Civil Rights Act (1964) and Voting Rights Act (1965). These events demonstrate the power of nonviolent resistance and civil disobedience. The movement faces violent opposition but achieves significant legislative victories. The events show the courage of activists and the importance of grassroots organizing.
Key Figures: Martin Luther King Jr. (civil rights leader), John Lewis (SNCC leader), Diane Nash (SNCC leader), James Farmer (CORE leader), Fred Shuttlesworth (Birmingham leader), Ralph Abernathy (SCLC leader), Bayard Rustin (March on Washington organizer), A. Philip Randolph (labor leader), Roy Wilkins (NAACP leader), Whitney Young (Urban League leader).
