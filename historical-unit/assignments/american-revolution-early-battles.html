American Revolution - Early Battles

Historical Context

Setting: Massachusetts and surrounding colonies, 1775. The first shots of the American Revolution are fired, marking the transition from political protest to armed conflict.
What Happens: The Battles of Lexington and Concord (April 19, 1775) mark the beginning of armed conflict. Paul Revere's midnight ride warns the colonists. The British march to Concord to seize colonial weapons but face resistance from minutemen. The Battle of Bunker Hill (June 17, 1775) shows colonial determination despite British victory. The Siege of Boston begins, trapping British forces in the city. The Continental Army is formed under George Washington's command.
Key Figures: Paul Revere (messenger), William Dawes (messenger), Dr. Samuel Prescott (messenger), John Parker (minuteman captain), Thomas Gage (British general), William Howe (British general), George Washington (Continental Army commander), Joseph Warren (patriot leader), Israel Putnam (colonial general), Artemas Ward (colonial general).
