Ancient Greece - Literature and Drama

Historical Context

Setting: Ancient Greece, 800-300 BCE. The development of Greek literature and drama that established the foundations of Western literary tradition.
What Happens: Ancient Greece produces some of the most influential literature and drama in history. Homer's epics (Iliad and Odyssey) establish the epic tradition. Greek tragedy develops with playwrights like Aeschylus, Sophocles, and Euripides exploring themes of fate, justice, and human nature. Greek comedy emerges with Aristophanes satirizing society and politics. Greek historians like Herodotus and Thucydides establish historical writing. Greek poetry includes lyric poets like Sappho and Pindar. These literary achievements establish the foundations of Western literature and drama that continue to influence modern storytelling.
Key Figures: Homer (epic poet), Aeschylus (tragic playwright), Sophocles (tragic playwright), Euripides (tragic playwright), Aristophanes (comic playwright), Herodotus (historian), Thucydides (historian), Sappho (lyric poet), Pindar (lyric poet), Hesiod (poet), Theocritus (poet), Menander (comic playwright), Xenophon (historian), Polybius (historian).
